About Me

Self-taught developer with 20+ years of real-world experience behind the code

The Story

I spent over 20 years in customer-facing roles — retail, supermarkets, hospitality. That background taught me something that still shapes everything I do: listen properly, understand the actual problem, and solve the right thing.

I came to development through genuine curiosity and stubbornness. Self-taught from scratch — HTML clicked almost immediately, PHP followed within weeks, and Laravel came after that. Since 2019 I've been building and shipping real projects, working intensively and learning by doing rather than by watching tutorials.

I don't code just to code. I build things that serve a purpose — small business sites, community platforms, e-commerce builds, admin tools. Clean, functional, and maintainable. No unnecessary dependencies, no over-engineering, no buzzword-driven decisions.

I've also coordinated remote teams, managed client relationships, and handled the project management side of things — keeping work organised, tracked, and delivered without things falling apart. The people skills from 20 years of customer service turned out to be just as useful in tech as anywhere else.

If you need someone who turns up, does the work properly, communicates clearly, and doesn't disappear after delivery — that's what I'm here for.

What I Value

Clean Code

Code is read far more than it's written. Meaningful names, clear comments, consistent structure. Someone else should be able to pick it up without needing a guide.

Simple Solutions

No over-engineering. No unnecessary plugins or APIs. Solve the problem cleanly and don't add complexity that doesn't need to be there. Simplicity is a feature.

Getting Things Done

Consistent, reliable, and focused. If I say I'll do something it gets done. No drama, no excuses, no disappearing act after the invoice.

Honest Communication

Clear updates, no surprises, no padding. If something's a problem I'll say so early rather than letting it become a bigger problem later.

Attention to Detail

Naturally meticulous — in code, in communication, in delivery. The small things matter. Broken layouts, inconsistent naming, and sloppy documentation all get fixed.

Reliability

You can count on me to show up, do the work, and not require hand-holding. Built from 20+ years of being the person others relied on to get things sorted.
